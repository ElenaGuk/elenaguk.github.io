Independent research · neurodiversity & employment

Autistic migrants often get misread at work. I want to study why.

I'm autistic and a first-generation migrant. I want to study something I've run into myself: how autistic migrants get read at work, and why their skills often don't reach the people who hire.

When you're neurodivergent and also new to a country, two readings land at once: an employer's idea of how a "good" candidate communicates, and a new country's idea of how work is done. I think competence gets discounted through both at the same time. That's my argument. I've written it up as a proposal; I haven't tested it yet.

How to read it: the dashed frame is what a person can actually do. Their experience is read through two filters at once — an unfamiliar culture's and a neurotypical one — and each narrows what gets recognised, so only part reaches the hiring decision on the right. This is how I picture it: the hypothesis, not measured data. The loss is in the reading, not in the person.

The proposal

What I'm proposing to study

My argument, and what stage it's at

  • The argument. The skills are there. The distortion is in how they get read. My claim is that a cultural reading and a neurotypical reading, together, discount competence before it reaches a hiring decision. I also look at it from the employer's side — the real cost-benefit — using my background in strategic management.
  • Why it matters. Most equality law protects people once they're hired, not from being filtered out quietly at the entrance. If the mechanism is real, the first step is to name it.
  • Status. I've written a full proposal and submitted it for funding (ISRF). I went to the Neurodiversity in Business conference in London and made contacts there. It isn't funded, there are no findings yet, and I haven't started the study. What I'm looking for is a PhD place to do it properly.

Alongside this I'm putting together a casebook of real misreadings and how they got resolved — working title "Translation from Neurotypical". It isn't finished or published.

Method

How I work without an institution

I set the direction

Without a supervisor, I read recent work and current papers to find a question that's actually open and that I can do on my own.

I go to the source

First-hand accounts and primary literature, not second-hand summaries. The people who've hit a barrier know its shape best.

I check it twice

I check each step against published methods. I don't have a title or an institution behind me, so the work has to hold up on its own.

I put it in the open

I present where I can and share material people can use, so the work can be checked and used.
